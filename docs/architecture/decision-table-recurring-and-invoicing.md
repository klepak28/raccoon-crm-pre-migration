# Decision Table: Recurring and Invoicing

## Purpose
This table turns critical recurring-scheduler and invoicing behavior into explicit implementation rules.

## Decision posture
- Prefer source-backed behavior from `/docs/spec`.
- Where the source leaves operational gaps, this table makes an explicit architectural decision to prevent ambiguous implementation.
- If a rule is still not safely decidable from the current docs, it is marked `OPEN_QUESTION` in Notes.

---

## Decision table

| # | Trigger | Affected records | Allowed / Not allowed | Required side effects | Invariants preserved | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Create a recurring job from a normal job | Job, RecurringSeries, future occurrence Jobs | **Allowed** when recurrence is enabled on a job that does not violate recurrence constraints. **Not allowed** for estimates, segments, or jobs with more than one appointment. | Create one `RecurringSeries`; current job becomes occurrence 1; generate future concrete occurrence jobs inside the materialization horizon. | Recurring jobs are concrete jobs, not one job with many dates. | Source-backed. |
| 2 | Create a recurring job from dedicated recurring-job flow | RecurringSeries, occurrence Jobs | **Allowed**. | Create the same underlying recurring structure as normal job -> recurrence flow. | Dedicated flow and normal flow must converge to one recurrence model. | `OPEN_QUESTION`: whether product parity is fully identical field-by-field, but architecture should still converge to one backend model. |
| 3 | Materializer evaluates a recurring series | RecurringSeries, future occurrence Jobs | **Allowed**. **Not allowed** to create infinite rows up front. | Generate only future occurrences inside horizon: daily/weekly/monthly 1 year ahead, yearly 5 years ahead; record extension progress. | Never-ending recurrence is finite in storage at any one time. | Source-backed on horizon behavior. |
| 4 | Determine whether an occurrence is virtual or persisted | Job, RecurringSeries | **Decision**: occurrences are persisted as real Jobs once materialized into the forward horizon. **Not allowed** to treat in-horizon occurrences as virtual-only calendar projections. | Store occurrence as concrete job with job number and recurrence linkage. | Each generated occurrence has its own job page, schedule, and invoice path. | This resolves a must-fix ambiguity from the audit. |
| 5 | Create one future occurrence during materialization | Job, RecurringSeries | **Allowed** exactly once per intended occurrence slot per series rule version. | Persist one concrete Job with linkage to the series and occurrence identity metadata. | No duplicate occurrence generation for the same logical slot. | Reliability rule inferred from audit; needed for idempotency. |
| 6 | Edit one occurrence using `Only this job` | occurrence Job, optional exception record, activity history | **Allowed**. **Not allowed** to rewrite future occurrences or past occurrences. | Mark occurrence as exception; persist overridden values on the occurrence Job; optionally store normalized exception metadata for auditability. | One-off edits do not mutate the series rule or future generated jobs. | The persisted source of truth is the occurrence Job; separate exception metadata is optional but recommended. |
| 7 | Reschedule one occurrence only | occurrence Job, optional exception record | **Allowed**. **Not allowed** to renumber or replace the occurrence with a new occurrence identity. | Update scheduledStart/scheduledEnd on the occurrence Job; flag it as exception if derived from a series; keep series linkage intact. | Rescheduling a single occurrence preserves occurrence identity and does not rewrite future generation. | Explicit decision to break ambiguity from audit. |
| 8 | Reassign one occurrence only | occurrence Job, activity history | **Allowed**. **Not allowed** to rewrite assignees for future occurrences. | Update assignment on the occurrence Job; move schedule placement; log assignment activity; mark exception if assignment diverges from series-driven default. | Single-occurrence assignee changes remain local. | Consistent with `Only this job`. |
| 9 | Edit `This job and all future jobs` | RecurringSeries, pivot occurrence Job, future occurrence Jobs | **Allowed**. **Decision**: keep one series identity and create a new internal rule version starting at the pivot occurrence. **Not allowed** to mutate historical occurrences. | Record new rule version at pivot; update pivot occurrence and eligible future occurrences; reconcile already-materialized future jobs to the new rule version. | Forward edits create a clear boundary at the selected occurrence and preserve history. | Chosen to avoid proliferating child series records while preserving traceability. |
| 10 | Edit the whole series from the beginning | RecurringSeries, future occurrence Jobs | **Allowed** only when the effective scope truly includes the series root. **Not allowed** to silently rewrite completed/invoiced historical occurrences. | Update the active recurrence rule from series start for non-protected occurrences; preserve protected historical jobs. | Historical operational/financial records remain stable. | Source does not explicitly name a UI path for “whole series”; this is an architectural rule for admin/backoffice operations if exposed. |
| 11 | Change recurrence date/time/assignee/arrival window for future scope | RecurringSeries, pivot occurrence Job, future occurrence Jobs | **Allowed**. | Apply new rule version from pivot occurrence forward; update or regenerate eligible future jobs within horizon. | Forward edits start at current occurrence and do not recompute earlier history. | Source-backed on allowed forward-propagating fields. |
| 12 | Change recurrence frequency or duration for future scope | RecurringSeries, pivot occurrence Job, future occurrence Jobs | **Allowed**. | Start the new rule at the pivot occurrence; update future materialization; if end date moves backward, delete/truncate future occurrences after the new boundary. | Series edits affect moving-forward history only. | Source-backed. |
| 13 | Delete `This Occurrence` | occurrence Job, activity history | **Allowed**. **Decision**: represent as a deleted/skipped occurrence marker, not as silent physical disappearance with no trace. | Remove the occurrence from normal scheduling surfaces; preserve enough metadata to prevent regeneration of the same slot. | One deleted occurrence does not truncate the series. | Needed so the materializer does not recreate a deleted slot. |
| 14 | Delete `This and Future Occurrences` | RecurringSeries, future occurrence Jobs | **Allowed**. | Truncate series at the selected occurrence boundary; remove or deactivate future unprotected occurrences; stop future materialization past cutoff. | Single-occurrence delete and tail truncation remain distinct operations. | Source-backed on scope semantics. |
| 15 | Cancel one occurrence | occurrence Job, activity history | **Allowed** as a distinct operational concept from delete. **Not allowed** to erase recurrence history. | Mark occurrence canceled; remove it from normal execution flow; keep historical visibility; prevent invoicing unless explicitly overridden later. | Cancel, delete, and unschedule remain distinct states. | Source is weaker here, so this is an explicit architecture decision. |
| 16 | Unschedule one occurrence | occurrence Job | **Allowed**. | Clear scheduled time fields or equivalent schedule state; preserve occurrence identity and recurrence linkage. | Unscheduling does not equal deletion. | `OPEN_QUESTION`: whether assignment should remain after unschedule. |
| 17 | Encounter an invalid monthly position such as 31st or 5th weekday in a month that lacks it | RecurringSeries, materializer | **Allowed** to skip. **Not allowed** to coerce to last valid date. | Do not create an occurrence for that month. | Monthly invalid-date behavior skips unsupported months. | Source-backed. |
| 18 | Determine how exceptions are stored | occurrence Job, optional exception metadata record | **Decision**: the occurrence Job is the operational source of truth; exception metadata may be stored as a diff/audit aid but must not be the only readable source for current operational state. | Persist edited values directly on the occurrence Job; optionally persist an exception descriptor. | Occurrence remains reconstructible even if future series rules change again. | Resolves audit concern about exception persistence. |
| 19 | Determine whether completed or invoiced occurrences can be rewritten by recurrence edits | occurrence Job, RecurringSeries | **Not allowed**. | Exclude completed/invoiced occurrences from recurrence rewrites and regeneration. | Financial and historical integrity are preserved. | Explicit protection added from audit. |
| 20 | User opens invoice flow for an occurrence/job | Invoice, occurrence Job | **Allowed**. | Create invoice on demand if no active baseline invoice exists; otherwise open the existing invoice. | Baseline mode does not create duplicate ordinary invoices for the same occurrence. | Explicit decision to resolve invoice creation timing. |
| 21 | Job becomes finished and customer has auto-invoicing enabled | Invoice, occurrence Job, AutoInvoiceRule | **Allowed** if invoice prerequisites are met. | Create and/or send the occurrence’s invoice according to auto-invoicing rule; do not create duplicates if baseline invoice already exists. | Auto invoicing is per customer and does not bypass duplicate-prevention rules. | Source-backed on auto-invoicing intent; timing decision made explicit here. |
| 22 | Determine whether an occurrence may have multiple invoices | Invoice, occurrence Job | **Allowed only** in progress-invoicing mode. **Not allowed** in baseline mode. | In baseline mode, enforce at most one active baseline invoice per occurrence. | Baseline one-occurrence-to-one-active-invoice rule holds. | Resolves major audit gap. |
| 23 | Determine whether one invoice may cover multiple occurrences | Invoice, occurrence Jobs | **Not allowed** in baseline recurring-job model. | Keep invoice rooted to one job/occurrence. | Each occurrence retains its own invoice lifecycle. | Strongly implied by source; recurring jobs generate separate jobs with separate invoices. |
| 24 | Generate invoice snapshot | Invoice, occurrence Job, Customer, Address, business settings, job line items | **Allowed** when invoice is created. | Copy customer-facing and business-facing render data into invoice-owned snapshot fields. | Invoice rendering does not drift when upstream records later change. | Snapshot must include all rendered content, not just totals. |
| 25 | Define minimum invoice snapshot content | Invoice, InvoiceLineItemSnapshot[] | **Required**. | Snapshot at least: customer-facing name, service address, business contact block, invoice date, due-term presentation, line items, option/job-input summary text shown on invoice, totals. | Printed/PDF invoice output remains stable. | Consolidates snapshot rule into one minimum set. |
| 26 | Edit invoice before send | Invoice | **Allowed**. | Mutate current editable invoice fields in place before any send event is recorded. | Pre-send invoice remains the working draft of that invoice. | Fits source evidence of invoice edit dialogs. |
| 27 | Edit invoice after send | Invoice, DeliveryEvent | **Allowed only with revision tracking**. **Not allowed** to overwrite the exact content tied to prior sends. | Create a new invoice revision or immutable render snapshot for subsequent sends/prints; preserve earlier send-linked render state. | Send history always points to the exact revision that was sent. | Explicit architecture decision because source leaves this open. |
| 28 | Apply payment to an invoice | Invoice | **Allowed**. | Update payment state and paid amounts; do not mutate historical sent revision content. | Document state and payment state stay distinct; amount due remains consistent. | Source-backed on separate payment behavior. |
| 29 | Customer name, address, or job display data changes after invoice creation | Customer, Address, Job, Invoice | **Allowed** on the source records. **Not allowed** to silently rewrite invoice-rendered values. | Update source records for future operational use only; retain relational links for navigation/reporting; keep invoice render fields frozen. | Invoice remains historically stable while still linked to current entities. | Explicitly resolves snapshot/reference boundary. |
| 30 | Customer/job data changes after invoice creation but before first send | Customer, Address, Job, Invoice | **Decision**: invoice snapshot stays frozen once invoice record exists unless the user explicitly edits/regenerates the invoice before send. | No silent re-sync; user must make an explicit invoice edit to update invoice-facing fields. | Invoice creation time remains the snapshot boundary. | Stronger but safer boundary than lazy re-snapshotting. |
| 31 | Attach files to invoice | Invoice, job file refs | **Allowed** only for files already on the job. **Not allowed** to attach arbitrary non-job files through this flow. | Store invoice attachment references to existing job files; expose them to customer when sent; do not embed them in PDF by default. | Invoice attachments remain references to job-owned files. | Source-backed. |
| 32 | Send invoice by email or text | Invoice, DeliveryEvent | **Allowed** by one channel per send action. **Not allowed** to send both email and text in one action. | Create a delivery event with channel, recipients, subject/message context, and timestamp. | Delivery history remains durable and channel-specific. | Source-backed. |
| 33 | Send invoice by text to multiple numbers | Invoice, DeliveryEvent | **Not allowed**. | Restrict SMS send to one number per action. | SMS send behavior remains consistent with source. | Source-backed. |
| 34 | Determine reminder eligibility | Invoice, InvoiceReminderRule | **Allowed** only when invoice has been sent, has a due date, is overdue, and remains unpaid; exclusions still apply. | Mark invoice eligible for reminder automation. | Reminder system remains separate from initial invoice creation/send. | Source-backed. |
| 35 | Determine auto-invoicing eligibility for a completed recurring occurrence | occurrence Job, Invoice, AutoInvoiceRule | **Allowed** if occurrence is finished, unpaid, customer has auto-invoice rule, and progress invoicing is not in use. | Create/send invoice under the customer-level auto-invoicing cadence. | Auto invoicing does not apply to progress invoices. | Source-backed plus explicit creation behavior. |

## Core invariants consolidated
- A recurring series is not one job with many dates.
- Every materialized occurrence is a concrete Job.
- In-horizon occurrences are persisted, not virtual-only.
- Single-occurrence edits do not rewrite the series.
- Forward edits create a clear boundary at the pivot occurrence.
- Deleted/skipped occurrences must not be regenerated accidentally.
- Completed or invoiced occurrences must not be rewritten by recurrence edits.
- In baseline mode, one occurrence may have at most one active baseline invoice.
- One invoice does not cover multiple recurring occurrences in the baseline model.
- Invoice-rendered data is invoice-owned snapshot data.
- Later customer/job changes do not silently rewrite historical invoices.
- Prior sends must remain tied to the exact invoice revision that was sent.
